Conservative Management in Traumatic Pneumothoraces in the Emergency Department (CoMiTED): A Randomised Controlled Trial


Guide to enrollment

Decide on Eligibility:

Inclusion Criteria:
  • Patients presenting with traumatic pneumothorax or pneumothoraces
  • Aged, or believed to be aged, 16 years and over, and
  • Treating clinician believes either a chest drain or conservative management would be a suitable initial treatment option
Exclusion Criteria:
  • Treating clinician(s) believe injuries are incompatible with life
  • Respiratory arrest
  • Haemothorax requiring a chest drain
  • Clinical and imaging evidence of tension pneumothorax in either lung
  • Prisoners (NB: “prisoners” does not include those in police custody; only those detained in prison establishments), and

If the patient is eligible:

Most eligible patients will temporarily lack capacity to fully consent to research.
In such cases they can be auto-enrolled and the research team will gain consent later.

If the patient has capacity; gain verbal agreement in principle before proceeding. Explain:

  1. Taking part in research is likely to improve the quality of their care
  2. Risks are carefully controlled - research is safe
  3. Explain the uncertainty that exists; for example, we do not know if their condition is best managed with usual care or the alternative under investigation
  4. If they change their mind they can withdraw their participation at any point

Actions to take to enroll the patient:

In standard working hours; 9-5
  • Please contact the ED Research Team when eligible patients arrive in ED:
    Send the ED Research Team a message via Epic chat "ED research Team" or phone 217907

Out of hours and weekends:
  • First select an option: Does the patient have capacity to consent?

For the patient with capacity:

  1. If you are on the delegation log, continue with enrollment.
    If you are not on the delegation log, check the Sharepoint folder: 'Research ⇨ Comited ⇨ Comited SIV register of attendance... ' to see if anyone on shift is.
  2. Provide the Patient Information Sheet and / or Video:
    3. CoMiTED patient information pages

    QRc link to patient information page. Show this on your phone to your patient.



  3. If the patient has capacity but is physically unable to sign, e.g. due to injuries, arrange for a witness to sign the consent - can be any relative or staff member.
  4. Once consented randomise the patient: Randomisation:
    https://www.sealedenvelope.com/access/

    5. The username and password for Sealed Envelope is on the front page of the per patient paperwork packs


  5. Document using the SMARTPHRASE .COMITEDTRIAL.
  6. Sign the ‘Screening Form’
    The form can be found in the rack in the ambulance bay next to the automatic doors.
  7. Place the Screening form in the Letterbox in ED reception (on the wall behind the receptionists)
  8. Send the ED Research Team an EPIC message with the patient details attached

For the patient who does not have full capacity:

  1. Two doctors are required to assess eligibility: 1 on the Delegation Log PLUS 1 not on the Delegation Log
    If you are not on the delegation log, check the Sharepoint folder: 'Research ⇨ Comited ⇨ Comited SIV register of attendance... ' to see if anyone on shift is.
  2. Go to the following website to randomise the patient:
    3. https://www.sealedenvelope.com/access/

    The username and password for Sealed Envelope is on the front page of the per patient paperwork packs


  3. Document SMARTPHRASE in notes (.COMITEDTRIAL)
  4. Sign the ‘Screening Form’ – both doctors need to sign the last page where prompted. The form can be found in the rack in the ambulance bay next to the automatic doors.
  5. Send the ED Research Team an EPIC message with the patient details attached
  6. Place the Screening form in the Letterbox in ED reception (on the wall behind the receptionists)
  7. Send the ED Research Team an EPIC message with the patient details attached
