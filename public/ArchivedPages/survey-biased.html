BiasED - unconscious bias

The Emergency Department Research Team invite you to participate in the BiasED study.

Please take time to carefully read the participant information below and discuss it with others if you wish. Please do not hesitate to ask us if there is anything that is not clear, or if you would like more information.

This study aims to explore if ED patient-facing staff (Doctors, Nurses and ED Technicians) have implicit (unconscious) racial bias by using a psychometric test called the IAT (Implicit Association Test). We are hoping that this work might inform ongoing discussion regarding structural racism in healthcare settings, but also promote individual self-reflection.

The IAT is an online psychometric test that measures strength of associations between concepts (i.e., colour of skin / sexual preference) and evaluations or stereotypes (i.e., good/bad, violent/clever). It does this by asking you to quickly sort words and pictures into categories and takes approximately 15 minutes from start to finish. At the end of the study test, you will receive a score indicating your level of subconscious preference towards black or white people.
