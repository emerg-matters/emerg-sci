PROcalcitonin and NEWS2 evaluation for Timely identification of sepsis and Optimal use of antibiotics in the Emergency Department.

Inclusion Criteria:

  1. All patients 16 years or older presenting to ED with suspected Sepsis

Exclusion Criteria:

  1. Already on IV Antibiotics
  2. Receiving chemotherapy
  3. Organ/stem cell/bone marrow transplant in last 3 months
  4. Patient requiring urgent surgical intervention
  5. Full DNACPR

Adherence:

The ultimate responsibility for clinical care of the patient lies with the treating clinician. The aim of the trial is to assess whether the use of PCT can improve decision making about which patients receive antibiotics and in what time period. If the study shows that PCT-supported assessment is superior to current practice, then it will lead to better outcomes in sepsis, improved care and a reduction in inappropriate antibiotics prescriptions.

The primary aim is to compare PCT- supported assessment with standard care of suspected sepsis in adults presenting to the ED, and measure whether this approach reduces prescriptions of antibiotics without increasing mortality by decreasing uncertainty in the group who may not need IV antibiotics urgently within 1 hour, or not need antibiotics at all.
