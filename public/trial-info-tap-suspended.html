A Prospective, Multicenter, Randomised, Double-Blind, Placebo-Controlled, Large Simple Trial Evaluating the use of BE1116 (4-factor Prothrombin Complex Concentrate (Kcentra/Beriplex)) to improve survival in patients with Traumatic Injury and Acute Major Bleeding.



TAP has been suspended temporarily.
We'll make an announcement if/when it restarts.

Eligibility

Local study conflicts

  • Patients enrolled into SWIFT by prehospital team cannot be enrolled to TAP - contact the prehospital team to establish status if necessary before proceeding. Take care especially if any blood products were given.

Inclusion Criteria:

  • Estimated or actual age > 16 years and Estimated or Actual weight > 50kg
  • Traumatic injury with a confirmed or suspected acute major bleed
  • Activation of massive transfusion protocol
  • Initiation of blood product transfusion (Whole Blood or Red Blood Cells or Platelets or Plasma has started to enter the patient’s body)
  • Anticipated start of IP infusion, 2 within 90 minutes after arrival at the hospital.

Exclusion Criteria:

  • Healthcare professional cardiopulmonary resuscitation including chest compressions for ≥ 5 consecutive minutes at any time before randomization
  • Isolated penetrating or blunt cranial injury, or exposed brain matter
  • Known anticoagulation treatment or a history of a TEE, within the past 3 months
  • Isolated burns estimated to be > 20% total body surface area or suspected inhalational injury
  • Ground level fall
  • Drowning or hanging
  • Normal level of consciousness (Glasgow Coma Scale score of 15)
  • Subject transferred from another hospital
  • Known heparin-induced thrombocytopenia
  • Known hereditary disorders that significantly increase prothrombotic risk such as hereditary antithrombin III deficiency, Factor V Leiden, etc.
  • Known or suspected pregnancy
  • Known anaphylactic or severe systemic reactions to BE1116 or any components in BE1116 including heparin, Factors II, VII, IX, X, Proteins C and S, antithrombin III, and human albumin.
  • Prisoner
  • Known “Do Not Resuscitate” order
  • Known participation in another interventional clinical study except with prior documented agreement from the sponsors
  • Known treatment with any prohibited medications including 3F-PCC, 4F-PCC, aPCC (FEIBA), and/ or Factor VIIa after trauma and prior to randomization.
  • Subject or Legally Acceptable Representative voiced an objection to participation in the trial
  • Moribund patient with devastating injuries expected to die within 1 hour of hospital arrival

If the patient is eligible:

Step by Step Guide to Recruitment:

In standard working hours; 9-5

Please contact the ED Research Team when eligible patients arrive in ED:
Send the ED Research Team a message via Epic chat "ED research Team" or phone 217907

Out of hours and weekends:

You must have completed the trial specific training and be on the delegation log to recruit patients to TAP

  1. Do not recruit if the patient has already been recruited to the SWIFT trial (EHAAT & MAGPAS) – Nb: EAAA are not recruiting to SWIFT trial.
  2. Confirm with the Trauma team leader that they are happy for the patient to be enrolled.
  3. Confirm that someone can administer Beriplex or has reviewed the online training
  4. Prescribe the TAP order set on EPIC and ensure IV access. (Not IO)
  5. Obtain the TAP pack from where it sits on top of the research cupboard in resus (It's on the wall near the blood fridge)
  6. Complete the Transfusion Lab Request Slip located in the pack – take it with you to the Blood Bank (Only a doctor or nurse)
  7. Collect the box of treatment from the Blood Bank on Level 3 – go to the window on the right when you walk in and press the doorbell - and ensure it is the same Subject Number as on the request slip.
  8. Return to ED - Remove the 2 paper forms in the envelope on the box and complete the boxes as you progress through the next steps.
  9. Document the time the box is opened on the above ‘Administration Form’
  10. Estimate the weight of the patient
    ≤ 75kgs – prepare 2 vials
    ≥ 75kgs - prepare 3 vials

  11. Administer the infused drug over 10-15 minutes (10 minutes if 2 vials, 15 minutes if 3 vials) - (Not through the Belmont). Do not use an IO line.
  12. Complete the 2 forms from the box - Administration form & Kit Usage form
  13. Ask the TTL or any Dr (someone who is NOT on the Delegation Log or affiliated with the trial) to sign Informed Consent Statement form as Professional Legal Representative
  14. Place all paperwork and the empty box on Susie’s desk in the top portacabin (Research Office) (far end, on the left as you walk in) Code C1256X
