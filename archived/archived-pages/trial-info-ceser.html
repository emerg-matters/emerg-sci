Looking at whether the signs and symptoms that patients have can be used to predict which patients have cauda equina compression on MRI

Eligibility

Local study conflicts

  • None

Inclusion Criteria:

  • Aged 18 years or over
  • They have a suspected diagnosis of cauda equina syndrome, defined by a clinician who routinely assesses such patients and considers it appropriate to organise an MRI to confirm or exclude this diagnosis
  • They are willing and able to give informed consent

Exclusion Criteria:

  • The MRI scan for suspected cauda equina compression had been undertaken before the clinical assessment
  • There is a suspected spinal fracture
  • There is evidence that the patient will be unable to adhere to trial procedures or participate in follow-up, including life expectancy of less than 12 months

If the patient is eligible:

Step by Step Guide to Recruitment:

In standard working hours; 9-5

  • Contact the ED Research Team when an eligible patient arrives in the ED — send a message via Epic chat to "ED Research Team" or phone 217907
  • The ED Research Team will see the patient to arrange consent

Out of hours and weekends:

  • The trial is currently only recruiting within the working hours of the research Team
